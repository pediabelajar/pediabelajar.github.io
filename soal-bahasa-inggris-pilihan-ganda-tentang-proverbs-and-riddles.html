1. Mention the grammatical structure of proverbs and riddles


STRUCTURE OF RIDDLE

Riddle have no form set. Riddles can be a question with a quick witty answer. They can be just a sentence that makes you have a sudden realization. We can also create riddles from words that rhytme with a word in a riddle’s answer, other word and phrases that use the funny part of an answer.

STUCTURE OF PROVERBS

Proverbs often from clusters that share a common structure.

– Opposite parallel

– Similiar parallel

– Single statement

– Statement with an explanation

– Comparison

– Descriptive list

– Use of clause or instruction

1. PROVERB (PRIBAHASA)

PROVERB atau dikenal dengan istilah pribahasa merupakan ayat atau kumpulan kata mempunyai susunan tetap, mengandung pengertian tertentu berisi saran-saran positif

Kinds of Proverbs:

1.      Aphorism (Pepatah)

This proverb offers advice.

Example                        : Don’t go too far in small.

(Persoalan kecil jangan dibesar-besarkan).

2.   Parable (Perumpamaan)

That has a moral lesson and has parable.

Example                       : To carry coals to New-castle.

(Bagai membuang garam kelaut)

3.   Slogan (Pameo)

This proverb gives spirit or motivation.

Example                       :

1.      Early bird gets worm

(Tuhan akan memberikan rezeki kepada makhluk-Nya yang lebih dulu bangun)

2.      Diligence is the mistress of success.

(Kerajinan pangkal kesuksesan)

4.   Idiom

Idiom adalah kelompok kata yang dirangkai dengan susunan tertentu dimana artinya tidak dapat ditebak dari arti kata-kata penyusunnya secara terpisah.

Example :

1.      Pull your sock up. (improve your behaviour)

2.      You can arrange your bedroom at will. (sesuka hati)

3.      Don’t mention it. (You’re welcome)  

2. RIDDLE (TEKA-TEKI)

         RIDDLE adalah jenis puisi yang menggambarkan sesuatu tanpa benar-benar penamaan apa itu, meninggalkan pembaca untuk menebak.  

Teka-teki bisa apa saja, dari teka-teki tentang binatang sampai tentang obyek. Tidak ada aturan tentang bagaimana struktur sebuah puisi teka-teki, teka-teki bisa lucu atau dapat sajak, itu tergantung pada orang yang menulis teka-teki.

7. [PROVERBS] - Bahasa Inggris Peminatan​


UNDERSTANDING THE CONTEXT

Task 2

1) A person who helps you when you really need help is a true friend [ 1. A friend in need is a friend indeed]

2) An argument is never only one person's fault. [ 7. It takes two to make a quarrel ]

3) Don't be upset about making a mistake, since you cannot undo. [ 8. It's no use crying over split milk]

4) Everyone has a chance to shine in the end. [ 10. Everyday has its day]

5) It is easier to find fault in other people than do it properly [ 9. Everyone can find fault, few can do better ]

6) Most of the people who talk a lot and are always teaching others is usually do not do much work [ 2. Great talkers are little doers ]

7) One who doesn't get what he needs is frustrated person and will lose their temper easily [ 6. A hungry man is an angry man]

8) People are not grateful for they have until they lose it. [ 5. We never miss the water until the well runs dry]

9) You can not only depend on divine help but must do something yourself to get what you want [4. God helps to those who help themselves]

10) You're never too old to change your way. Learning is always possible. [3. It's never too late to mend]

EXPLANATION

Proverb atau Peribahasa adalah sekumpulan petuah atau wejangan bagaimana dalam menjalani kehidupan yang bermakna. Pada umumnya proverb menggunakan majas/bahasa kiasan di balik makna sebenarnya.

BERDASARKAN SOAL YG DITANYA

1) A friend in need is a friend indeed

Meaning : A person who helps you when you really need help is a true friend.

[Teman sejati adalah seseorang yang selalu ada ketika kamu membutuhkannya]

2) Great talkers are little doers

Meaning : Most of the people who talk a lot and are always teaching others is usually do not do much work

[Tong kosong bunyinya nyaring. Orang-orang yang banyak ngomong dan selalu mendikte orang lain biasanya praktiknya nihil]

3) It's never too late to mend

Meaning : You're never too old to change your way. Learning is always possible.

[Nggak ada kata terlambat untuk belajar dan memperbaiki diri menjadi lebih baik. Bahwa sesungguhnya manusia dilahirkan untuk terus belajar selama hidupnya.

4) God helps those who help themselves

Meaning : You can not only depend on divine help but must do something yourself to get what you want.

[Jangan hanya mengandalkan bola datang, berusahalah mengejarnya sampai dapat. Bahwa sesungguhnya Allah meridhoi orang-orang yang mau bekerja keras atas rezeki mereka]

5) We never miss the water until the well runs dry

Meaning : People are not grateful for they have until they lose it.

[Orang yang tidak pernah bersyukur, akan selalu merasa kurang dan kurang sampai pada suatu masa mereka menyadari kehilangannya]

6) A hungry man is an angry man

Meaning : One who doesn't get what he needs is frustrated person and will lose their temper easily.

[Seseorang yang tidak bisa mendapatkan apa yang diinginkan akan menjadi frustasi dan bahkan hilang akal sehatnya]

7) It takes two to make a quarrel

Meaning : An argument is never only one person's fault.

[Lihatlah sisi yang lain sebelum menilai kesalahan orang]

8) It's no use crying over split milk

Meaning : Don't be upset about making a mistake, since you cannot undo.

[Nasi telah menjadi bubur, kesalahan yang telah diperbuat, tidak dapat disesali, namun kita memiliki kesempatan untuk memperbaikinya ke depannya]

9) Everyone can find fault, few can do better.

Meaning : It is easier to find fault in other people than do it properly

[Lebih gampang mencari kesalahan orang lain daripada berusaha mencairkan situasinya]

10) Everyday has its day

Meaning : Everyone has a chance to shine in the end.

[Bumi terus berputar, setiap orang memiliki kesempatan emas. Kehidupan seseorang terus berubah seiring berputarnya waktu]

Jawaban:

1. "Two wrongs don't make a right." When someone has done something bad to you, trying to get revenge will only make things worse.

2. "The pen is mightier than the sword." Trying to convince people with ideas and words is more effective than trying to force people to do what you want.

3. "When in Rome, do as the Romans." Act the way that the people around you are acting. This phrase might come in handy when you're traveling abroad notice that people do things differently than you're used to.

4. "The squeaky wheel gets the grease." You can get better service if you complain about something. If you wait patiently, no one's going to help you.

5. "When the going gets tough, the tough get going." Strong people don't give up when they come across challenges. They just work harder.

6. "No man is an island." You can't live completely independently. Everyone needs help from other people.

7. "Fortune favors the bold." People who bravely go after what they want are more successful than people who try to live safely.

8. "People who live in glass houses should not throw stones." Don't criticize other people if you're not perfect yourself.

9. "Hope for the best, but prepare for the worst." Bad things might happen, so be prepared.

10. "Better late than never." It's best to do something on time. But if you can't do it on time, do it late.

11. "Birds of a feather flock together." People like to spend time with others who are similar to them.

12. "Keep your friends close and your enemies closer." If you have an enemy, pretend to be friends with them instead of openly fighting with them. That way you can watch them carefully and figure out what they're planning.

13. "A picture is worth a thousand words." Pictures convey emotions and messages better than written or spoken explanations. That's why Phrase-Mix has illustrations :)

14. "There's no such thing as a free lunch." Things that are offered for free always have a hidden cost.

15. "There's no place like home." Your own home is the most comfortable place to be

PROVERB

Sebelum membuat 10 soal pilihan ganda mengenai proverb, mari kita belajar memahami apakah itu proverb?

Proverb adalah kalimat singkat yang isinya berupa nasehat, biasanya berlandaskan pada common sense atau pengalaman.

Proverb juga dipengaruhi oleh agama dan budaya. Dalam bahasa Indonesia sehari-hari, proverb disebut sebagai peribahasa.

Berikut ini contoh soal mengenai proverb dan jawabannya:

1. All that glitters is not ____

a. Silver

b. Gold

c. Emerald

d. Diamond

Answer: b. Gold

2. The eyes are the _____ of the soul

a. Window

b. Door

c. View

d. Eyes

Answer: a. Window

3. Beauty is in the eye of the _____

a. Look

b. Beholder

c. Behold

d. Holder

Answer: b. Beholder

4. Behind a great man there is a ____ woman

a. Wise

b. Good

c. Great

d. Independent

Answer: c. Great

5. Better safe than ____

a. Dangerous

b. Sorry

c. Save

d. Late

Answer: b. Sorry

6. Better late than____

a. Sorry

b. Never

c. Ever

d. Dangerous

Answer: b. Never

7. Every cloud has a _____ lining

a. Gold

b. Dark

c. Silver

d. Red

Answer: c. Silver

8. Which proverb below means that someone who doesn't understand how to save money will spend it quickly.

a. Golden key can open any door.

b. Good talk save the food.

c. Fortune favors the brave

d. A fool and his money are soon parted.

Answer: d. A fool and his money are soon parted

9. Which proverb below means something better must follow an unpleasant event:

a. After a storm comes a calm

b. A change is as good as a rest

c. A dog is a man's best friend

d. A leopard can't change its spot

Answer: a. After a storm come a calm

10. Which proverb below means what you do is more important

a. A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step

b. Action talk louder than words

c. A penny saved is a penny earned

d. A person is known by the company he keeps

Answer: b. Action talks louder than words

The answer riddles matched the riddles:

Why is water like a horse? → They both run.What has four legs and flies? → A dirty horse.It grows down. → A beard.It has legs but it cannot walk. → A table.Half of it is sand, but people eat it. → A sandwich.

Simak penjelasan berikut.

Pembahasan

Untuk mencocokkan pertanyaan teka-teki dari jawaban yang tersedia. Kita pahami terlebih dulu artinya. Arti dari pertanyaan terkadang mengandung makna tertentu atau makna kiasan.

Sebelum menjawab pertanyaan, kita pahami pilihan dari jawaban.

A table = sebuah meja.They both run = mereka berdua lari.A sandwich = roti lapis daging.Seekor kuda yang kotor.

Berikut terjemahan pertanyaan dan penjelasan jawaban tiap teka-teki di atas.

1. Mengapa air seperti seekor kuda?

Air dan kuda sama-sama melakukan gerakan 'run'. 'Run' yang dimaksud untuk kuda adalah berlari. Sedangkan, 'run' yang dimaksud untuk air adalah mengalir, bergerak (berjalan) cepat. Dengan demikian, air dan kuda sama-sana 'run', they both run.

2. Apa yang memiliki 4 kaki dan lalat?

Pertanyaan ini agak menjebak. Yang memiliki 4 kaki tentunya adalah hewan. Tetapi memilliki lalat juga. Maksudnya adalah ada lalat di tubuhnya. Maka, jawaban yang paling sesuai adalah kuda yang kotor, a dirty horse.

3. Ini tumbuh ke bawah.

Apa yang tumbuh ke bawah? Tentunya sesuatu yang memanjang ke bawah. Jawaban yang paling tepat adalah jenggot, beard.

4. Memilili kaki dan tidak bisa berjalan.

'Kaki' pada soal tersebut memiliki makna kiasan. Kaki yang dimaksud adalah kaki meja, karena kaki meja tidak bisa berjalan. Jawaban yang paling tepat adalah a table.

5. Setegah dari itu adalah pasir, tetapi orang-orang memakannya.

Satu-satunya nama makanan yang diawali dengan sand adalah sandwich.

24. 3. Proverbs and ridles can be categorized into four (positive statement, negative statement, imperative, and parallel phrases). Please categorize the following proverbs and riddles (Score 20): - Every cloud has silver lining - Nothing venture, nothing gained - Don't flog a dead horse - You cannot study for this test - Out of the frying pan into the fire


Jawaban:

- Every cloud has silver lining (positive statement)

- Nothing venture, nothing gained (parallel phrases)

- Don't flog a dead horse (imperative)

- You cannot study for this test (negative statement)

- Out of the frying pan into the fire(negative statement)

Penjelasan:

Every cloud has silver lining (positive statement)

Positive statement berarti memiliki arti yang positif, proverb "Every cloud has silver lining " berarti "dalam setiap keburukan pasti terdapat hikmah" yang menjadikan proverb ini sebagai positive statement.

Nothing venture, nothing gained(parallel phrases)

Proverb parallel phrases biasanya memiliki kesamaan kata pada kedua frasa yang terdapat di dalamnya. Pada proverb "Nothing venture, nothing gained", kata "nothing", tertulis pada frasa pertama dan terulang pada frasa kedua sehingga menjadikannya sebuah proverb parallel phrases.

Don't flog a dead horse (imperative)

Imperative berarti memiliki ajakan, proverb "Don't flog a dead horse ", memiliki arti "jangan melakukan hal yang sia-sia", yang merupakan ajakan untuk tidak melakukan suatu hal yang tidak berarti sehinggan proverb "Don't flog a dead horse " merupakan proverb imperative.

You cannot study for this test (negative statement)

Riddle "You cannot study for this test " memiliki arti yang negatif, dalam kasus ini, riddle "You cannot study for this test "  secara harfiah berarti "anda tidak bisa belajar untuk tes ini" yang mengimplikasikan bahwa anda tidak bisa mempersiapkan persiapan untuk suatu hal.

Out of the frying pan into the fire(negative statement)

"Out of the frying pan into the fire" berarti suatu kondisi yang buruk telah menjadi lebih buruk lagi. Arti dari proverb ini menunjukkan sesuatu yang tidak baik atau negatif sehingga proverb "Out of the frying pan into the fire" adalah proverb negative statement.

36. [PROVERBS] - Bahasa Inggris Peminatan​


EXPLANATION

Proverb atau Peribahasa adalah sekumpulan petuah atau wejangan bagaimana dalam menjalani kehidupan yang bermakna. Pada umumnya proverb menggunakan majas/bahasa kiasan di balik makna sebenarnya.

BERDASARKAN SOAL YG DITANYA

1) Every cloud has a silver lining

Meaning : You should never feels hopeless because difficult times are always followed by better days

Badai pasti berlalu. Hari baik pasti tiba di balik kesulitan yang ada.

2) Money is the root of all evil

Meaning : All wrongdoing is because of wanting wealth

Menghalalkan segala cara selalu ditempuh bagi mereka yang ingin cepat kaya.

3) When in Rome do as the Romans do

Meaning : When visiting a foreign land, follow the customs of those who live in it.

Pintar-pintarlah beradaptasi di manapun langkah berpijak

4) Beggars can't be choosers

Meaning : People who are in great need must accept any help that is offered, even it's a not complete solution to their problems.

Jangan menolak kesempatan yang ada jika tidak ada pilihan yang lain

5) Beauty is in the eye of the beholder

Meaning : Different people will find different things beautiful and the differences of opinion don't matter too much

Setiap orang memiliki tujuan masing-masing.

6) A barking dog seldom bites

Meaning : Those who make loud threats seldom carry them out

Tong kosong bunyinya nyaring. Orang yang suka menggertak, nihil praktiknya.

7) Don't put all your eggs in one basket

Meaning : You should not focus all resources on one hope or possibility, because it is too risky.

Jangan mengerahkan semuanya pada satu harapan jika tidak ingin kecewa nantinya.

8) Too many cooks spoil the broth

Meaning : If too many persons are managing an activity, they can easily ruin it.

Jika terlalu banyak yang ikut campur dalam menyelesaikan pekerjaan, jangan harap pekerjaan tersebut berjalan lancar.

9) A leopard can not change its spots

Meaning : One can't change one's essential nature

Jika sudah menjadi bagian dari karakternya akan sulit untuk berubah

10) Curiosity killed the cat

Meaning : This proverb is used to warn against being too curious lest one should suffer harm

Terlalu penasaran akan sesuatu membahayakan diri sendiri
